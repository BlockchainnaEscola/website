Blockchain na Escola is pioneering Ethereum adoption in Brazil's most underserved regions—bringing public goods, DeFi, and Web3 to communities far from traditional crypto hubs.

Our Ethereum Mission

While most crypto education concentrates in São Paulo, Rio, and global tech centers, we're deliberately targeting:

  • Remote rural regions (Chapada Diamantina, Serra ES)
  • Peripheral urban communities (favelas in Salvador, Belo Horizonte)
  • Indigenous & quilombola communities (Liga Colaborativa dos Povos)
  • Public school students (traditionally excluded from Web3 opportunities)
  • Small cities (Seabra, Serra - far from crypto epicenters)

Why This Matters: Ethereum's promise of financial inclusion and public goods only works if we reach beyond the already-included.

Case Study: Chapada Diamantina

Remote Indigenous First

The Challenge

Chapada Diamantina is 6+ hours from Salvador. A region of indigenous communities, quilombola settlements, small farmers, zero previous crypto exposure.

The Result

  • 250 participants at first event (February 2025)
  • First blockchain club in rural Brazil
  • Students learning Ethereum fundamentals
  • Expansion to 27 cities across interior Bahia

Why This Is Different

We're teaching Ethereum to:

  • Students who've never left their small towns
  • Communities 6+ hours from nearest city
  • People whose first smartphone is their Web3 wallet
